Project Title: Implementing an Interpretable Machine Learning Model for Data Analysis

Previously, we would translate information from our reality into a binary representation so a computer could model real-world phenomena. As this usage of models continued they become more complex and harder to interpret. As such, "interpretability" (the ability for humans to understand the model) become a core issue in the field of machine learning. My dataset was the WISDM dataset, it recognised which physical activity a subject was performing. My goal was to make this dataset (which consisted of 104 features from accelerometer/gyrometer data) into something more interpretable. Techniques Involved: LIME, SHAP, PCA, Logistic Regression, HeatMaps, Predictive Features and more.

Project Title: Text-based Monopoly Game

Survivology is a simple Ecologically themed Monopoly Game that substitutes the fiat money of monopoly with environmentally-oriented currency.
A list of the environmental substitutions:
Currency: instead of money players gather "protectors", e.g. a chance card's reward could be 30 protectors. These protectors are under the jurisdiction of a player.
Areas: Each Area type represents a region of high biodiversity.
Squares: each square represents an endangered animal, e.g. Borneo Orangutan from the area "Borneo".
Continents: Each region (set of squares) represents a continent.
Houses: Houses are replaced with camps, the notion being you set up a camp on a square (Location of an endangered animal) to help with its preservation. Each additional camp requires more protectors. When an opposing player lands on a square with a camp, they must provide protectors which will further increase preservation efforts.
Sanctuaries: They're the equivalent of a monopoly hotel.
Passing Go: Represented by a complete circumnavigation of the world.
Jail: Jail is in Antartica. This is modelled after the fact that the south pole station is not accessible for half of the year due to extremely cold temperatures preventing planes from landing.
